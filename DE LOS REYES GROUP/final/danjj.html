Danjugan Island Marine Reserve and Sanctuaries
Location: Brgy. Bulata, Cauayan, Negros Occidental, Philippines
Open hours: Monday-Sunday 6:00AM - 5:00PM
Danjugan is a 43-hectare island rich in biodiversity. It has 5 lagoons, white sand beaches, bat caves, lush limestone and mangrove forests and surrounded by vast seagrass beds and fringing coral reefs. It is a wildlife and marine sanctuary in Southern Negros, Philippines.
Marine & Wildlife Camp in Danjugan Island, an island sanctuary with forests, lagoons and reefs teeming with an incredible diversity of terrestrial and marine life. For its size, it holds and incredible biodiversity- at least 72 bird species, 10 bat species, 22 butterfly species, 17 mangrove species, 572 fish species, have been recorded in the island.
